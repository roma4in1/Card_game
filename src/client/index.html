        Lock In — how to play

        Press your luck with 9 dice over 5 rounds. Most points wins.

        Your turn: roll all 9 dice and lock one target number (1–6) for the whole turn — one die of it is set aside automatically.

        Keep rolling: each roll, if any die shows your target you set aside exactly one. Roll two or more of it and you also pull a chip from your discard into play (if you have one).

        Bust or pay: roll zero targets and you must spend a chip to reroll — or your turn ends.

        Bank anytime after a successful roll: score +1 per die set aside. Set aside 8 → +1 bonus. All 9 → +3 (and a reserve chip moves into play); a perfect run with no chips spent → +5.

        Game end: +2 points per chip left in your play area. Highest total wins; ties broken by play-area chips.

          Yahtzee — how to play

          Fill all 13 categories over 13 rounds. Highest grand total wins.

          Your turn: roll five dice up to three times. Tap dice to keep them between rolls, then commit to exactly one unused category — that ends your turn.

          Upper (Ones–Sixes): sum of the dice showing that face. Reach 63+ up top for a +35 bonus.

          Lower: 3/4 of a kind (sum of all dice), full house (25), small straight (30), large straight (40), Yahtzee — five of a kind (50), chance (sum).

          Bonus Yahtzee: roll another five-of-a-kind after scoring a 50 in Yahtzee for +100 each. You must place it in the matching upper box, or (if used) as a Joker in any open lower box at full value.

          You must always score something — dump a bad roll as a 0 if you have to.

            Spy Game — how to play

            Every player shares one secret footballer — except the spy, who has a similar decoy.

            Detectives: you all share the same secret player. Over 3 rounds, each give a one-word clue about them — but don't be so obvious the spy works it out.

            Spy: you get a different but similar player. Blend in with plausible clues and avoid the vote.

            Vote: after each round you can call an early vote — if a majority agree, you accuse straight away; otherwise play continues (3 rounds max). Then everyone secretly votes for the spy. Most votes is caught (a tie means the spy escapes).

            If the spy is caught, they get one guess at the detectives' player from a shortlist — guess right and the spy steals the win; guess wrong and the detectives win.

              Codenames — how to play

              Two teams race to contact all their agents on a 5×5 board.

              Each team has one spymaster (who sees the secret key) and operatives (who don't).

              Spymaster: give a one-word clue and a number — how many cards it points to (e.g. “ANIMAL 3”).

              Operatives: tap cards you think are your team's. A correct agent lets you keep going (up to the number +1); a neutral or enemy card ends your turn.

              Reveal all your agents to win. Tap the assassin and you instantly lose.

                Quoridor — how to play

                Race your pawn to the opposite side. No luck, all strategy.

                On your turn, do one of two things: move your pawn one cell, or place a wall.

                Walls block movement for everyone. You can't fully wall a player off — every pawn must always keep at least one route to its goal.

                If an opponent sits in your way you may jump over them (or diagonally, if a wall or another pawn is behind them).

                First pawn to reach any cell on its goal edge wins. Walls are limited — spend them wisely.

                  Tectonic Shift — how to play

                  Slide pawns across a hex board, banking the tiles you leave behind.

                  On your turn, slide one pawn in a straight line — it can't cross a removed tile, another pawn, or the edge.

                  The hex you leave is removed and its value is banked to you (tiles are worth more toward the centre). Tiles you pass over are untouched.

                  Removing tiles fractures the board into islands. Isolate regions so only your pawns can harvest them — but you still must move hex-by-hex to collect.

                  A pawn with no move is stuck, and the tile it dies on is lost. Highest total wins; tiebreak is most pawns still able to move.

                    Memory Match — how to play

                    Match each word to its picture. Most pairs wins.

                    On your turn, flip two cards. A pair is a word card and the picture of the same thing (e.g. “apple” + 🍎).

                    Match → you keep the pair, score a point, and go again. Miss → the cards flip back and the next player goes.

                    Word cards show in your chosen language (English, French, or Korean) — switch anytime; everyone sees the same board, just their own language.

                    The game ends when the board is cleared. Most pairs wins; a tie is shared.

                      Who Am I? — how to play

                      One secret footballer. Ask yes/no questions the server answers from the data, then name them.

                      On your turn, either ask a yes/no question (position, nationality, continent, league, value, era, retired) — the server answers from the player's real data, and everyone hears the answer — or guess a name.

                      Correct guess wins the round. Wrong guess knocks you out for the rest of the round — so guessing is a real risk, not a free try.

                      A round ends when someone guesses right, or everyone's been knocked out (nobody wins). Most round-wins takes the match; fewest questions breaks a tie.

                        Guess the Player — how to play

                        Wordle for footballers. Everyone hunts the same secret player; fewest guesses wins.

                        Guess any real player. Each guess is graded per attribute: green = match, yellow = partial (shares one), grey = miss.

                        The value column shows an arrow: ↑ the target is worth more than your guess, ↓ worth less, = same tier.

                        You all race the same target at your own pace — you only see your own grid (opponents show a guess count). First/fewest to solve wins the round; best over the match wins.

                          Penguin Knockout — how to play

                          Secretly aim & power up. Everyone launches at once — knock rivals off the shrinking ice.

                          Commit: drag from your penguin to aim — direction = where you slide, drag distance = power. Nobody sees your move until it resolves.

                          Resolve: all penguins launch simultaneously with real physics — friction, bouncing collisions, chain reactions. Shove someone off the ice for a knockout.

                          Careful: too much power and you overshoot the edge and eliminate yourself. After each round the ice shrinks — anyone stranded outside melts in.

                          Win: last penguin standing (+3) plus +1 per knockout. Highest total wins.

                            Ice Football — how to play

                            Two teams on ice. Secretly aim & power your player; everyone launches at once. Kick the ball into their goal.

                            Commit: drag from your player to aim, set power, optionally activate a banked power-up. Nobody sees your move until it resolves.

                            The ball is lighter & slicker, so kicks carry. Walls keep the ball in — it only leaves through a goal. Your players, though, slide right off the edge (and respawn next round).

                            Power-ups sit on the pitch — drive over one to bank it, spend it later: 💥 power shot, 🐘 big body, 🧊 slick, ❄️ freeze a rival, 🧱 wall.

                            First team to the goal target wins.
